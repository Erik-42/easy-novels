Novelist Quickstart Guide

Welcome to Novelist, the ultimate app for planning, writing, and organizing your novels. This guide will walk you through the basics so you can confidently start your storytelling journey.

1. Bookshelf: Your Library of Books

When you open the app, you’ll find your Bookshelf—a central hub for managing all your projects.

  • Create a book: Tap the “+” button. Choose the Novel standard template or a custom template based on an existing book.
  • Edit, set cover or delete book: Press the three dots menu for options.
  • Unlimited books: There’s no limit to how many books you can create!
  • Book safety indicators: Books with a green indicator are safe, while those with a red indicator need to be backed up to avoid losing your work.

Tap a book to access Novelist's main workspace.

2. Four Essential Sections

The core of Novelist is divided into Plot, Write, Organize, and Schedule, each serving a key purpose:

2.1 Plot: Plan Your Story’s Building Blocks

This is where you create and manage the core elements of your story: Themes, Characters, Events, and more.

  • Add items: Tap the “+” button to create a new item. Each item needs a title (required) and optionally a synopsis.
  • Customize categories: Create, edit, or delete categories to suit your writing style (e.g., add “Factions” or “Planets”).
  • Organize items: Use the status circle to track each item’s progress (e.g., First Draft, Done). Long press the circle to delete or merge items. Long press an item to reorder them.
  • Move items: Assign an item to a different category or split it into two.
  • Filter and search: Quickly find items using filters or keywords.
  • Detailed item view: Tap an item to edit its title, synopsis, names, tags, images, metadata, relationships, notes, and rich text descriptions.

2.2 Write: Draft Your Scenes

Scenes are the backbone of your novel, holding the actual content of your story.

  • Create scenes: Tap the “+” button to add a new scene with a title (required) and opptionally asynopsis.
  • Rich text editor: Write your story using formatting options like bold, italic, underline, and more. Mention items using the “@” symbol. Add links and comments.
  • Manage scenes: Attach Plot items, split scenes into two, or rearrange them in the list by long pressing.
  • Track progress: Use the status circle to keep tabs on each scene’s development.
  • Detailed scene view: Edit the title, synopsis, subplots, text, notes, images, and related Plot items.
👉 Important: Only text written in scenes will appear in your final manuscript.

2.3 Organize: Structure Your Novel

The Organize section helps you shape your book into a coherent structure, dividing it into acts, parts and chapters.

  • Add sections: Create a tree-like structure to break your book into chapters or other divisions.
  • Attach scenes: Choose scenes from the Write screen that take part in each section.
  • Rearrange sections: Easily reorder chapters or other subsections to adjust your book’s flow.
👉 Important: Only scenes attached to sections are included in the manuscript.

2.4 Schedule: Set Goals and Track Progress

Stay motivated with clear goals and track your writing progress over time.

  • Set goals: Choose a word count target, a deadline, or both.
  • Daily progress: See a chart of your word count each day to keep yourself accountable.

3. Preview and Export Your Book

  • Preview: Tap the eye icon to see how your manuscript will look when finished.
  • Export your book: Tap Export and share in the main menu to create your final manuscript in EPUB, ODT, or HTML format. Share it on the fly on available apps.

4. Backup and Sync: Safeguard Your Work

  • Backups: Save your data locally or to the cloud (Google Drive or iCloud).
  • Manual sync: Use Send to cloud and Receive from cloud to manually transfer content between devices.
👉 Important: Make frequent backups to avoid losing your work due to device issues.

5. Settings: Personalize Your Writing Experience

  • Dark Mode: Enable this for comfortable writing in low-light conditions.
  • Explore other settings to tailor the app to your preferences.

6. Tips for New Users

  • Start small: Experiment with a short story or outline to get comfortable with the features.
  • Use templates: Start with the Novel standard template and customize as needed.
  • Preview regularly: Check your progress and structure.
  • Backup often: Don’t risk losing your hard work.

You’re now ready to start your writing adventure with Novelist! Take your time to explore each feature, and remember: your creativity is the only limit. Happy writing! ✍️
